About Me

I am an enthusiastic and driven sophomore majoring in Statistics and Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, with a knack for challenging myself, be it through taking tough classes, personal projects or online courses. My goal is to make use of my skills to give back to society, which I hope to reflect through my projects and work.
My passion lies in the fields of Data Science and Machine Learning, specifically in Deep Learning. To further my understanding of the field, I spent last summer as a Deep Learning Intern at Degirum, a disruptive startup in the bay area, and the exciting projects I've worked on has further fueled my passion for the subject.
Apart from college courses, I also take part in research under Professor Schneider. The past year, I worked on graphically representing semantic medical data using tools such as Neo4J, a graphical database management system. Based on my findings, I presented a poster at the Undergraduate Research Symposium last spring. I am currently working on writing a paper on Deep Learning that I hope to publish soon.
